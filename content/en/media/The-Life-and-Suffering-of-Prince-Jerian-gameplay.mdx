## 🏛️ Introduction

Welcome to the comprehensive guide for "The Life and Suffering of Prince Jerian." This narrative RPG from Schisma Games and 101XP takes you on a journey through the political and personal struggles of Prince Jerian. As the heir to the throne of the Blessed Arknian Empire, Jerian faces difficult decisions that will shape his destiny and the future of his realm.

## 📚 Overview

### 🌐 Game Mechanics

"The Life and Suffering of Prince Jerian" is a narrative-driven RPG that focuses on political choices and their consequences. The game features a complex political landscape where Jerian must navigate the intricacies of power, alliances, and rivalries. Your decisions will influence the story's direction, leading to multiple possible endings.

### 🏛️ Setting

The game is set in the fictional world of the Blessed Arknian Empire, a realm where politics, religion, and personal beliefs intertwine. The game's setting is rich in detail, with various locations to explore and characters to meet.

### 🎮 Gameplay

The gameplay is driven by choices. Players will encounter multiple decision points where they must choose between different options, each with its own consequences. These decisions can affect relationships, political outcomes, and the story's direction.

## 📜 Walkthrough

### 🏛️ Chapter 1: The Heir

In the first chapter, you'll meet Prince Jerian and learn about his background. You'll make your first decision, choosing whether to attend a royal banquet or stay in your chambers.

### 🏛️ Chapter 2: The Council

In Chapter 2, Jerian must attend the royal council. Here, you'll face your first major political decision, choosing between supporting a proposed law or opposing it.

### 🏛️ Chapter 3: The Rebellion

In Chapter 3, a rebellion breaks out. You must decide how to handle the situation, whether by negotiating with the rebels or taking a more aggressive approach.

## 🤝 Choices and Consequences

Your choices in "The Life and Suffering of Prince Jerian" have significant consequences. The game's multiple endings reflect the different paths you can take based on your decisions. Some endings may be bittersweet, while others may be tragic or triumphant.

Once you select a class in "The Life and Suffering of Prince Jerian," the decision is permanent. However, you can create a new character if you want to explore a different class.

Yes, but it won't be as effective as choosing a class that complements that playstyle, like Knight or Rogue.
